# qualitative research

[[Qualitative research]] is a method of inquiry that explores human experiences, perceptions, and motivations through non-numerical data. It aims to understand complex phenomena by gathering detailed insights from participants, often using interviews, focus groups, or open-ended surveys.

Anthropic conducted one of the largest and most multilingual qualitative studies on artificial intelligence (AI) usage, engaging over 80,000 users from 159 countries and 70 languages. The study collected rich, user-centered data about individuals’ hopes, fears, and experiences with AI technologies, emphasizing real-world perspectives beyond abstract or theoretical debates.

The research identified nine primary themes representing users’ desires for AI, including professional excellence, personal transformation, societal impact, and creative expression. This thematic categorization revealed a nuanced coexistence of optimism and concern regarding AI’s role in daily life. Participants ranged across diverse professions and geographies, such as healthcare workers and freelancers in the USA, entrepreneurs in Nigeria, Honduras, and Cameroon, software engineers in Mexico, Poland, France, and South Korea, managers and executives in Denmark, lawyers in Israel, and technical support specialists in the USA.

By focusing on qualitative data, Anthropic’s study highlights the complex emotional and practical dimensions of AI adoption, providing valuable insights for developers, policymakers, and researchers interested in the human factors shaping AI integration.
