# AI in Warfare

Frontier AI models entering the national security and military domain. As of May 2026, a Pentagon cyber official described advanced AI models — naming [[entities/anthropic]]'s **Mythos** specifically — as potentially "fundamentally changing warfare," especially in cyber operations (Source: [[sources/S — Top Tech News May 15 2026]]).

## Why Cyber First

Cyber operations are the lowest-friction adoption path for advanced AI in warfare:
- **Identification** — automated scanning for vulnerabilities at scale
- **Reasoning** — chaining exploits, planning intrusion paths
- **Exploitation** — code generation tuned to specific CVE patterns
- Same capability used **defensively** (patching faster) and **offensively** (shortening discovery-to-exploit path)

The dual-use property is the core tension — frontier models built for general capability cross into weapons-grade territory without retraining.

## Tensions

- **Anthropic's safety framing vs Pentagon's offensive framing** — Mythos is positioned by its maker as a safety-focused frontier model. Singled out by a defense official as warfare-grade. Whose framing wins shapes both regulation and commercial positioning. See [[entities/anthropic]].
- **Disclosure vs concealment** — government wants visibility into frontier capabilities; labs face commercial and safety reasons not to publish.
- **Export controls** — frontier models could face the same regimes as advanced chips. Compliance becomes a product feature.
