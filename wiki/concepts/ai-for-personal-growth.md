# AI for personal growth

Anthropic conducted the largest multilingual qualitative study on AI usage, gathering insights from over 80,000 users across 159 countries and 70 languages. This research explored users’ hopes, fears, and experiences with AI, revealing a diverse range of aspirations related to [[AI for personal growth]]. The study identified nine primary themes in users’ desires for AI, including professional excellence, personal transformation, societal impact, and creative expression.

The findings highlight a complex coexistence of optimism and concern about AI’s role in individuals’ lives. Users from various professions—such as healthcare workers, freelancers, entrepreneurs, software engineers, managers, lawyers, and technical support specialists—shared nuanced perspectives that go beyond abstract debates, focusing instead on concrete, real-world applications of AI.

AI tools, including those powered by Anthropic’s Claude models, are increasingly seen as enablers for personal development by assisting with skill enhancement, creativity, productivity, and emotional well-being. However, the study also underscores anxieties about privacy, ethical use, and the potential for dependency on AI technologies.

This research contributes to a deeper understanding of how AI intersects with personal growth, emphasizing the importance of designing AI systems that align with diverse user needs and values. It also encourages ongoing dialogue about balancing innovation with responsible AI use in everyday life.
