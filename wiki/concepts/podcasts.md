# podcasts

Podcasts are digital audio programs that users can stream or download, often organized as episodic series covering a wide range of topics. In educational contexts, podcasts have become a popular medium for delivering content in an accessible and engaging format.

Adobe's AI-powered study tool, [[Acrobat Spaces]], incorporates AI-generated podcasts as part of its suite of features designed to assist students. These podcasts are automatically created from various document types, enabling learners to consume study materials audibly. This integration positions podcasts as a complementary resource alongside other AI-driven study aids such as presentations, flashcards, and quizzes.

The inclusion of AI-generated podcasts within Acrobat Spaces reflects a broader trend of leveraging artificial intelligence to enhance educational tools. By converting text-based content into audio formats, these podcasts support diverse learning preferences and improve accessibility. Adobe developed Acrobat Spaces with input from students at universities like Harvard, Berkeley, and Brown, aiming to create a centralized hub for study materials without requiring user login.

Adobe's offering competes with similar AI-enhanced educational products such as Google's [[NotebookLM]] and [[Goodnotes]], which also integrate various study aids but may differ in features like podcast generation. The use of AI to produce podcasts exemplifies how audio content is evolving beyond traditional recording methods to become dynamically generated and personalized.
