# AI grounding in source documents

AI grounding in source documents refers to the process by which artificial intelligence systems generate outputs—such as summaries, presentations, or quizzes—that are directly based on and verifiable against the original input materials. This ensures that AI-generated content remains accurate, relevant, and traceable to the source documents, reducing misinformation and enhancing trustworthiness.

Adobe's Acrobat Spaces exemplifies AI grounding by enabling students to create study materials from diverse document types while maintaining a clear connection to the source content. Launched as a free, AI-powered study tool, Acrobat Spaces integrates features like chat assistance and AI-generated podcasts that rely on the underlying documents for context and factual accuracy. This grounding approach allows users to interact with AI outputs that are anchored in the original texts, supporting effective learning and content creation.

Developed with feedback from students at leading universities such as Harvard, Berkeley, and Brown, Acrobat Spaces aims to be a comprehensive hub for academic study without requiring user login. By hosting the tool on a separate URL, Adobe encourages widespread adoption and ease of access. Acrobat Spaces competes with similar AI-driven study platforms like Google's NotebookLM and Goodnotes, all of which emphasize grounding AI outputs in source documents to enhance educational utility.

AI grounding in source documents is critical in educational tools to ensure that AI-generated content supports learning objectives accurately and transparently. By linking AI responses to verifiable source material, tools like Acrobat Spaces help maintain academic integrity and foster user confidence in AI-assisted study environments.
