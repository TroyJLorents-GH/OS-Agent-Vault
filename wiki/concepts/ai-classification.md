# AI classification

AI classification refers to the categorization of user needs and expectations regarding artificial intelligence, based on qualitative research into how diverse populations interact with and perceive AI technologies. A notable example is the largest multilingual study conducted by [[Anthropic]], which gathered insights from over 80,000 users across 159 countries and 70 languages. This research aimed to understand the real-world hopes, fears, and experiences people have with AI, moving beyond theoretical debates to capture concrete user perspectives.

The study identified nine primary themes representing users' desires for AI, encompassing areas such as professional excellence, personal transformation, societal impact, and creative expression. These themes illustrate a complex coexistence of optimism and concern about AI’s role in individuals’ lives. Participants included a wide range of professionals, such as healthcare workers, freelancers, entrepreneurs from Nigeria, Honduras, and Cameroon, software engineers from Mexico, Poland, France, and South Korea, managers and executives from Denmark, lawyers from Israel, and technical support specialists from the USA.

Anthropic’s research utilized [[Claude]] and Claude-powered classifiers to analyze qualitative data, enabling a nuanced classification of user aspirations and anxieties. This approach highlights the multifaceted ways AI is integrated into personal and professional contexts worldwide, emphasizing the importance of understanding diverse user needs to guide responsible AI development and deployment.
