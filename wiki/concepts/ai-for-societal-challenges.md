# AI for societal challenges

Anthropic conducted the largest and most multilingual qualitative study on AI usage, collecting insights from over 80,000 users across 159 countries and 70 languages. The study explored users' hopes, fears, and experiences with AI, revealing a diverse range of perspectives on how AI intersects with societal challenges.

The research identified nine primary themes reflecting users' desires for AI, including professional excellence, personal transformation, societal impact, and creative expression. These themes illustrate a complex coexistence of optimism and concern regarding AI’s role in individual lives and broader society. Participants ranged from healthcare workers and freelancers in the USA to entrepreneurs in Nigeria, Honduras, and Cameroon, as well as software engineers in Mexico, Poland, France, and South Korea, among others.

By emphasizing real-world aspirations and anxieties, the study moves beyond abstract debates about AI ethics and governance to highlight concrete user perspectives. This approach underscores the importance of understanding AI’s societal impact through the lived experiences of diverse global communities.

Anthropic’s findings contribute to ongoing discussions about how AI technologies, such as [[Claude]] and [[Claude-powered classifiers]], can be developed and deployed responsibly to address societal challenges while respecting user concerns and expectations.
