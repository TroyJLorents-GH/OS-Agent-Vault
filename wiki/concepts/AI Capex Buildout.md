# AI Capex Buildout

The 2026 capital cycle treating AI infrastructure (chips, memory, networking, data centers, energy, cooling, land) as **strategic infrastructure** rather than software-multiples investments. Investors increasingly value pure-play AI infra companies on multi-year capacity commitments, not quarterly subscription revenue.

## Datapoints (May 2026)

- **Cerebras IPO** — $5.5B raise, ~$95B closing market cap, +68% day 1 (Source: [[sources/S — Top Tech News May 15 2026]])
- **Nvidia** — $5.5T intraday market cap; broad-market proxy for AI infra demand
- **Argentum AI** — $2.5B data center deal with cloud + real estate partners
- **Kioxia** — $8.2B Q1 profit on memory chip demand from AI servers
- **Samsung memory** — record bonuses (up to 607% salary) for memory chip division
- **Gartner forecast (2026):** $2.52T global AI spending, +44% YoY, driven by inference/deployment (Source: [[sources/S — AI Technology Trends 2026 Key Developments]])

## Why It Looks Like Infrastructure, Not Software

- Long-lead-time energy + cooling contracts
- Multi-year compute commitments
- Capital allocation looks more like utilities than SaaS
- Public market valuations (Cerebras, Nvidia) price strategic position, not near-term cash flow

## Constraints Tightening

- **Memory bandwidth** — inference needs HBM; supply tied to Samsung / SK Hynix / Kioxia / Micron
- **Energy** — local grid limits, water for cooling
- **Land + permits** — [[AI Data Center Backlash]] is making sites harder to secure
- **Labor** — Samsung 18-day strike (planned May 21) **averted** by a last-minute government-mediated deal (May 20, ~74% union approval); supply-side fragility was real but contained this round, and chip-vs-non-chip pay tensions persist. See [[entities/Samsung]]
- **Sovereign chip access** — Huawei/GLM-5 demonstrated zero-Nvidia training is possible; opens parallel cycle
