### Model Selection Guide

- **GPT-5.4** — General excellence, structured output, tool integration, computer-use
- **[[Claude]] Sonnet 4.6** — Coding, writing quality, agency workflows, long-form content (128K token output), content pipelines with strict style guides
- **Gemini 3.1 Pro** — Multimodal reasoning (text/image/audio/video/code), cost-effective ($2/M output tokens), Deep Research feature
- **Grok 4.20** — Real-time web data, multi-agent cross-verification, X platform integration
- **Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite** — High-volume, cost-sensitive workloads ($0.25/M input tokens)
- **Llama 4 / open-source** — Self-hosted deployments, data sovereignty, cost control at scale

## Key Trends

- **Model-agnostic architecture** is now a requirement, not a nice-to-have. Hard-coding a specific model is technical debt that compounds monthly.
- **Multi-model orchestration** replacing single-model dependence. Route tasks to the right model, not the "best" model.
- **Open-source reaching parity** with commercial: [[Mistral]], [[DeepSeek]], [[Meta AI]] (Llama 4) match frontier benchmarks at a fraction of API cost.
- **Pricing collapse:** Frontier-level performance increasingly affordable. Gemini Flash-Lite at $0.25/M input tokens sets a new floor. Cost-per-intelligence improving faster than almost any other stack factor.
- **[[Agentic AI]] infrastructure** maturing: MCP at 97M installs, Agentic AI Foundation under Linux Foundation, every major provider shipping MCP-compatible tooling.
- **Computer-use agents** becoming standard across all frontier models — shift from "AI coding assistants" to "AI software developers."
- **Vibe coding** — building apps through natural language — named 2026 breakthrough by MIT Technology Review.

## Structural Shifts

### The "Two Paths" Bifurcation

The AI market is splitting into two distinct tracks:
1. **Elite enterprise computation** — massive-scale models (10T+ params) for high-stakes tasks (cybersecurity, drug discovery, financial modeling)
2. **Democratized lightweight tools** — cost-optimized models making frontier-adjacent capability accessible to startups and small teams

### From Answers to Actions

The center of competition has moved from "AI that answers questions" to "AI that gets things done." The full competitive chain is now: hold long context, make plans, use tools, verify results, finish the task. (Source: [[S — New AI Model Releases April 2026 Startup Edition]])

### Inference Is the Bottleneck

A Google researcher and a Turing Award winner identified inference (not training) as the real crisis in AI. Hardware was never designed for inference at current scale. Whoever solves this reshapes the economics of AI deployment. (Source: [[S — New AI Model Releases April 2026 Startup Edition]])
