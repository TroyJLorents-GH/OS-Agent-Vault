# sentiment analysis

Sentiment analysis is the computational process of identifying and categorizing opinions expressed in text to determine the writer's attitude, emotions, or subjective information. It is widely used to understand user perspectives and emotional responses, especially in the context of artificial intelligence (AI) applications.

Anthropic conducted one of the largest and most multilingual qualitative studies on AI usage, gathering insights from over 80,000 users across 159 countries and 70 languages. This research explored users' hopes, fears, and experiences with AI, revealing a complex coexistence of optimism and concern about AI’s role in their lives. The study categorized users' primary desires for AI into nine main themes, including professional excellence, personal transformation, societal impact, and creative expression.

By analyzing this extensive and diverse dataset, sentiment analysis tools—such as those powered by Anthropic’s Claude classifiers—help capture nuanced, real-world aspirations and anxieties people have about AI. This approach moves beyond abstract debates to concrete user perspectives, providing valuable insights for developers, policymakers, and stakeholders aiming to align AI technologies with human values.

The study included participants from various professions and regions, such as healthcare workers and technical support specialists in the USA, freelancers in multiple countries, entrepreneurs in Nigeria, Honduras, and Cameroon, software engineers in Mexico, Poland, France, and South Korea, managers and executives in Denmark, and lawyers in Israel. This diversity underscores the global relevance and applicability of sentiment analysis in understanding AI’s societal impact.

Sentiment analysis thus plays a crucial role in interpreting qualitative data on AI usage, enabling a better grasp of the emotional and practical implications of AI technologies across different cultures and industries.
