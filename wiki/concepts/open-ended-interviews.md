# open-ended interviews

Open-ended interviews are a qualitative research method used to gather in-depth insights by allowing participants to express their thoughts, feelings, and experiences freely without being constrained by predefined answer choices. This approach facilitates the exploration of complex and nuanced perspectives, making it particularly valuable in studies examining human attitudes and behaviors.

A notable application of open-ended interviews is found in the largest and most multilingual qualitative study on AI usage conducted by [[Anthropic]]. The study engaged over 80,000 users from 159 countries and 70 languages, collecting rich, detailed responses about their hopes, fears, and experiences with artificial intelligence. By employing open-ended questions, researchers were able to identify nine primary themes reflecting users’ desires for AI, including professional excellence, personal transformation, societal impact, and creative expression.

The flexibility of open-ended interviews enabled the study to capture a complex coexistence of optimism and concern regarding AI’s role in people’s lives. This method moved beyond abstract debates, providing concrete, real-world user perspectives that highlight the nuanced aspirations and anxieties surrounding AI technologies.

Participants in the study represented diverse professions and regions, such as healthcare workers in the USA, freelancers, entrepreneurs from Nigeria, Honduras, and Cameroon, software engineers in Mexico, Poland, France, and South Korea, managers and executives in Denmark, lawyers in Israel, and technical support specialists in the USA. This diversity underscored the method’s effectiveness in eliciting varied and culturally contextualized insights.

Open-ended interviews remain a cornerstone technique in qualitative research, especially when the goal is to understand complex human experiences and attitudes, such as those related to emerging technologies like AI.
