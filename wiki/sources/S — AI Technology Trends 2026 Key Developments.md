## Summary

A state-of-the-industry survey arguing that 2026 is the inflection point where AI transitions from experimentation to scaled deployment. The article organizes the 2026 landscape around five structural shifts: the trillion-dollar spending pivot, agentic AI breakthrough, multimodal integration, physical AI, and the regulatory wave. Introduces the key framing of "[[Inference Economics]]" as the new center of gravity for the AI industry.

## Key Claims and Findings

### The Trillion-Dollar Pivot

- Global AI spending projected to exceed **$2.02 trillion in 2026** (Gartner's higher estimate: **$2.52T, +44% YoY**)
- Corporate AI investments hit **$581.7B in 2025** (+130% YoY per Stanford AI Index)
- **87% of companies** report AI reduced annual costs; **30% saw >10% revenue increase** (PwC)
- New framing: **"[[Inference Economics]]"** — spending shifting from training to running models at scale
- The era of experimentation is over; we're in the age of scaled deployment

### Agentic AI Breakthrough

- **Terminal-Bench:** AI agent success on real-world tasks jumped from **20% (2025) to 77.3% (April 2026)**
- **GPT-5.4** and **[[Claude]] Opus 4.6** released Feb 2026 with advanced agentic frameworks
- Claude Opus 4.6: **1 million token context window** — processes entire codebases or full contract sets
- **SAP Q1 2026:** 40+ specialized Joule agents for extracting business requirements
- See [[Agentic AI]] for deeper treatment

### Multimodal AI

- **Gemini 3.1 Ultra** processes hours of video + cross-references text reports, generates insights in seconds
- No longer bolted-on modules — natively integrated text, image, audio, video

### Physical AI

- **[[NVIDIA]] GTC 2026:** Isaac GR00T open models for humanoid robots
- **WORKR:** AI lets operators deploy robotic arms without programming
- **CES 2026:** autonomous vehicles were the dominant theme
- **Waymo, Zoox** expanding robotaxi services; **Uber** launching autonomous ride service
- **NVIDIA Alpamayo** physical AI platform — **BMW and Ford** as partners
- **Ford Level 3** (hands-off driving) by 2028
- See [[Physical AI]] for full treatment

### Regulation Wave

- **EU AI Act** high-risk obligations from **August 2, 2026**
- **California AI Transparency Act** + **Generative AI Training Data Transparency Act** (effective Jan 1, 2026)
- **Colorado AI Act** (effective June 30, 2026)
- Rising demand for **"Explainable AI" (XAI)**
- See [[AI Regulation]] for full regulatory map

### Structural Shifts

- **88% of organizations** already use AI in at least one function (McKinsey)
- Microsoft's 2026 vision: human-AI collaboration with AI "co-pilots"
- "Managing intelligent agents will soon become a core skill for most professionals"
- **"Great Divergence"** in adoption: manufacturing/logistics prioritize AI; content sectors face a **"Crisis of Distinctiveness"**
