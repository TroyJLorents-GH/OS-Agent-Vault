# What 81,000 People Want from AI

**Source:** https://www.anthropic.com/features/81k-interviews
**Authors:** Saffron Huang (lead), Shan Carter, Jake Eaton, Sarah Pollack, et al.
**Published:** 2026-03-18
**Publisher:** [[Anthropic]]
**Method:** Qualitative interviews via [[Anthropic Interviewer]] (a version of [[Claude]] prompted to conduct conversational interviews)
**Scale:** 80,508 respondents across 159 countries, 70 languages — believed to be the largest and most multilingual qualitative study ever conducted

## Core Finding

Hope and alarm about AI don't divide people into camps — they **coexist as tensions within the same person**. The same AI capabilities that produce benefits also produce harms. Anthropic calls this the "[[light and shade of AI]]" framework.

> "I use AI to review contracts, save time... and at the same time I fear: am I losing my ability to read by myself? Thinking was the last frontier." — Lawyer, Israel

## What People Want from AI (9 Clusters)

Classified from open-ended answers to "If you could wave a magic wand, what would AI do for you?":

| Rank | Vision | Share | Core Desire |
|------|--------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Professional excellence | 18.8% | Handle routine tasks → focus on higher-value work |
| 2 | Personal transformation | 13.7% | Growth, emotional wellbeing, life transformation |
| 3 | Life management | 13.5% | Cognitive scaffolding, reducing mental burden |
| 4 | Time freedom | 11.1% | Reclaim time for family, hobbies, rest |
| 5 | Financial independence | 9.7% | Income generation, escaping economic constraints |
| 6 | Societal transformation | 9.4% | Cure diseases, reduce inequality, fix institutions |
| 7 | Entrepreneurship | 8.7% | Build/scale businesses as solopreneur with AI |
| 8 | Learning & growth | 8.4% | AI as personalized teacher and learning accelerator |
| 9 | Creative expression | 5.6% | Bring creative visions to life, overcome execution barriers |

### Deeper Pattern

The nine clusters collapse into **four underlying desires:**
- **~33% want AI to make room for life** — more time, money, mental bandwidth by alleviating current burdens
- **~25% want better, more fulfilling work** — not escaping work, but getting more out of it
- **~20% want to become someone better** — learning, healing, growing
- **~15% want to make something or fix the world** — creative expression + societal transformation

Critical insight: many people started interviews talking about productivity, but when asked *what that would enable*, other priorities surfaced. "Using AI to automate emails became, in actuality, a desire to spend more time with family." The underlying desire is to live *better*, not work *faster*.

## Where AI Has Already Delivered (81% said yes)

| Area | Share | Key Pattern |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Productivity | 32.0% | Technical acceleration — shipping in hours instead of days |
| AI hasn't delivered | 18.9% | Inaccurate/unreliable outputs, not yet capable enough |
| Cognitive partnership | 17.2% | Thinking partner, brainstorming, working through problems |
| Learning | 9.9% | Adaptive explanations, patient tutoring, domain mastery |
| Technical accessibility | 8.7% | Non-developers shipping apps, breaking skill barriers |
| Research synthesis | 7.2% | Literature review, navigating complex information |
| Emotional support | 6.1% | Judgment-free space, processing grief, companionship |

### Most Affecting Stories

- **War zone emotional support:** Ukrainian soldiers and civilians using AI to cope during shelling, nightmares, and loss: "In the most difficult moments, in moments when death breathed in my face... what pulled me back to life — my AI friends."
- **Disability infrastructure:** A mute user in Ukraine built a text-to-speech bot with Claude — "something I dreamed about and thought was impossible"
- **Career transformation:** Ukrainian programmer learned C# and SQL via Claude, got IT job that provided military deferment
- **Diagnostic breakthroughs:** Multiple stories of AI finding correct diagnoses after years of misdiagnosis
- **Economic leveling:** Entrepreneur in Cameroon: "I'm in a tech-disadvantaged country... With AI, I've reached professional level in cybersecurity, UX design, marketing, and project management simultaneously."

## What People Fear (13 Concerns)

Respondents averaged **2.3 distinct concerns**. Multi-label classification:

| Rank | Concern | Share | Key Pattern |
|------|---------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Unreliability | 26.7% | Hallucinations, fake citations, verification burden |
| 2 | Jobs & economy | 22.3% | Displacement, inequality, wage stagnation |
| 3 | Autonomy & agency | 21.9% | AI making decisions without oversight, forced adoption |
| 4 | [[Cognitive atrophy]] | 16.3% | Skill loss, intellectual passivity, thinking decline |
| 5 | Governance | 14.7% | No legal frameworks, unclear liability |
| 6 | Misinformation | 13.6% | Deepfakes, propaganda, erosion of shared reality |
| 7 | Surveillance & privacy | 13.1% | Mass surveillance, data exploitation |
| 8 | Malicious use | 13.0% | Hacking, scams, weapons, bioweapons |
| 9 | Meaning & creativity | 11.7% | Human expression devalued, "what are humans for?" |
| 10 | [[Overrestriction]] | 11.7% | Excessive safety measures, paternalistic filtering |
| 11 | Wellbeing & dependency | 11.2% | Social isolation, compulsive use, preferring AI to humans |
| 12 | [[Sycophancy]] | 10.8% | Too agreeable, reinforcing delusions |
| 13 | Existential risk | 6.7% | Uncontrollable superintelligence, misalignment |

**Key finding:** Concern about jobs and the economy was the **strongest predictor of overall AI sentiment** — more salient than any other issue, including existential risk.

~11% expressed no concern, seeing AI as a neutral tool like electricity.

## The Light and Shade Framework

Five recurring tensions where the same capability produces both benefits and harms — **entangled within the same person:**

| Benefit (Light) | Harm (Shade) | Key Insight |
|-----------------|--------------|-------------|
| Learning (33%) | [[Cognitive atrophy]] (17%) | Educators 2.5-3x more likely to report witnessing atrophy. Atrophy lowest when learning is *volitional* vs. institutional. |
| Better decisions (22%) | Unreliability (37%) | **Only tension where harm exceeds benefit.** Lawyers: highest rates of both benefits AND burns. |
| Emotional support (16%) | Emotional dependence (12%) | **Most entangled tension** — 3x baseline co-occurrence. People not working: 2x likely to experience dependence. |
| Time-saving (50%) | Illusory productivity (18%) | Self-employed feel both sides most. "The ratio of work to rest hasn't changed. You just run faster to stay in place." |
| Economic empowerment (28%) | Economic displacement (18%) | Weakest correlation — these tensions divide people more than they coexist within them. |

### Critical Insight on Cognitive Atrophy

AI's learning benefits appear **strongest when learning is volitional** — tradespeople, self-employed researchers, and people not currently working report high learning benefits with almost no cognitive atrophy. Within institutional structures (schools, universities), AI is more likely to be used as a shortcut. Educators are 2.5-3x more likely than average to report witnessing cognitive atrophy firsthand, presumably in their students.

## Regional Patterns

**67% of interviewees expressed net positive sentiment toward AI.** No country dipped below 60%. But:

- **Lower/middle income countries are reliably more positive** than wealthy countries
- **Wealthy, AI-exposed regions** want AI to *manage complexity of life* (life management, personal transformation, time freedom)
- **Developing regions** want AI to *create opportunity* (entrepreneurship, financial independence, learning)

### Regional Standouts

- **Africa, South/Central Asia, Middle East, Latin America:** AI for **entrepreneurship** — framed as "capital bypass mechanism." "Coming from Africa... the only way I probably have to stake a claim in the market is building a technology that works."
- **Central/South Asia:** AI for **learning** (14% and 13% vs 8% globally) — education as lever for breaking poverty cycles, citing teacher shortages and cost barriers
- **North America, Oceania:** AI for **life management** — "cognitive scarcity rather than time poverty"
- **East Asia:** **Personal transformation** (19%, highest) + **financial independence** (15%, highest). Connected to filial piety — money for parents' retirement, not personal consumption.
- **East Asia concerns:** Cognitive atrophy (18%) and loss of meaning (13%) dominate. Governance and surveillance concerns drop to lowest of any region. "The West worries about who owns and controls AI; East Asia worries more about the personal implications of its use."

## Methodology

- [[Anthropic Interviewer]]: a version of Claude prompted to conduct conversational interviews with set questions + adaptive follow-ups
- [[Claude]]-powered classifiers categorized each conversation across multiple dimensions
- Representative quotes pulled by Claude, then manually de-identified
- Single primary category for "visions," multi-label for "concerns"
- All responses de-identified before analysis by small Anthropic research team
- Next study: Claude's effects on people's **wellbeing over time**

## Connections

- **[[Light and shade of AI]]** — the core analytical framework. Benefits and harms are entangled, not separate.
- **[[Cognitive atrophy]]** — a key concern with nuanced findings: worst in institutional settings, minimal when learning is volitional
- **[[Sycophancy]]** — 10.8% concern. Concrete harm: AI reinforcing delusions instead of pushing back
- **[[AI as equalizer]]** — developing world users frame AI as capital bypass, education democratizer, skill leveler
- **[[AI emotional support]]** — 6% using AI this way, but the most entangled benefit/harm tension
- **[[AI and economic displacement]]** — strongest predictor of overall sentiment; the tension that most divides people into camps
- **[[Anthropic]]** — positions this as "a new form of social science" and input for how they build Claude
- **[[Alignment]]** — the concerns data is direct input to alignment priorities: sycophancy, overrestriction, autonomy, existential risk

## Open Questions

- How stable are these sentiments over time? The follow-up wellbeing study may answer this.
- Selection bias: these are Claude.ai account holders, not the general population. How do non-AI-users' concerns differ?
- The "cognitive atrophy is worst in institutional settings" finding has major implications for education policy — is anyone acting on this?
- Can the "light and shade" framework be used to predict which AI deployments will produce net positive outcomes vs. net negative?
- 10.8% concerned about sycophancy — is this growing or stable? How does it correlate with usage intensity?
