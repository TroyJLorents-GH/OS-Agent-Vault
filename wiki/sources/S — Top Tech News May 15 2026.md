## Macro Frame

The 2026 AI boom is exposing cracks across the system: labor, energy, geopolitics, antitrust, national security. Stories cluster into five themes — **infrastructure capex**, **labor displacement & friction**, **AI security/warfare**, **platform fights**, **public backlash**.

## Stories — Synthesized

### Compute & Infrastructure
- **[[entities/Cerebras]] IPO** — wafer-scale chipmaker priced at $185, opened at $350 (+89%), closed +68% at $331.07. ~$5.5B raised. Fully diluted valuation ~$106.75B; closing market cap ~$95B. Largest US tech IPO since Uber (2019). First pure-play public bet on alternatives to [[entities/NVIDIA]].
- **[[entities/NVIDIA]] $5.5T intraday market cap** — Nvidia rally pushed major indexes to fresh records. Investors treating AI infrastructure as multi-year buildout cycle (chips, memory, networking, data centers, energy).
- **Argentum AI $2.5B data center deal** — signed with cloud + real estate partners. AI infra is now valued like strategic infrastructure, not software.
- **Kioxia $8.2B Q1 profit** — memory chip demand from AI servers. Memory bandwidth becoming a hard cost driver for inference, not just GPUs. See [[Inference Economics]].

### Labor & Workforce
- **[[entities/Samsung]] 18-day strike May 21** — 45,000+ workers. Trigger: bonus disparity — memory division gets up to 607% annual salary bonuses (AI demand), logic/foundry gets 50-100%. Analyst estimates $14-21B operating profit hit. Strike threatens memory chip supply to AI data centers globally.
- **[[entities/Multiverse]] $70M raise → $2.1B valuation** — London ed-tech for AI workforce training. Adjacent market: AI reskilling, internal training, productivity expectations.
- **Class of 2026 job market** — entry-level tech roles compressed. Internships, junior roles, support functions, analyst work increasingly automated. Career ladder into tech is breaking.

### AI Security & Warfare
- **Pentagon cyber official calls advanced AI "revolutionary warfare"** — singled out [[entities/anthropic]]'s **Mythos** model as potentially "fundamentally changing warfare," especially cyber operations. See [[AI in Warfare]].
- **Microsoft Exchange zero-day** — exploited in attacks; Exchange remains high-value target tied to identity, comms, sensitive data.
- **Pwn2Own Berlin 2026 day 1** — $523K paid; 24 unique zero-days; Windows 11 + Edge among targets.
- **White House cyber official:** identity security is the AI-era control point. Agent access to internal systems = board-level risk.

### Platform Fights
- **Musk v. OpenAI trial wraps** — Oakland federal court. [[entities/Elon Musk]] seeking **$150B in damages** to nonprofit arm + removal of [[entities/Sam Altman]] and Greg Brockman. Musk side accused Altman of lying; [[entities/OpenAI]] side called Musk "selectively amnesiac," noting he knew about for-profit plans since 2018 and wanted control over Tesla or xAI. [[entities/Microsoft]] defended as responsible investor. Jury deliberates Monday. Could force restructuring as OpenAI eyes $1T IPO.
- **[[entities/OpenAI]] vs [[entities/Apple]]** — OpenAI reportedly exploring legal options over Siri partnership (ChatGPT integration shipped 2024). Tension over who controls the user relationship: platform owner vs model provider.
- **OpenAI Codex on mobile** — ChatGPT iOS/Android can now manage Codex tasks across laptops, dev boxes, remote envs. AI coding agents → mobile control layer.
- **EU Android AI order** — proposal would force Android to give third-party AI services parity access with Gemini. Apple backs Google's pushback. Mobile OS = next AI distribution layer under [[AI Regulation]].

### Public Backlash & Policy
- **Gallup: 71% of US adults oppose local AI data center** — 48% strongly opposed. Energy, water, noise, land use. See [[AI Data Center Backlash]].
- **US-China AI guardrail talks begin** — Trump administration opens dialog with Beijing. Framed in same category as chip controls and nuclear-era coordination.
