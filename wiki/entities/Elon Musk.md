# Elon Musk

Co-founder of [[entities/OpenAI]]; founder of xAI, Tesla, SpaceX. As of May 2026 the principal litigant against OpenAI for allegedly abandoning its nonprofit charter.

## Musk v. OpenAI (Oakland Federal Court)

- Filed against OpenAI and [[entities/Sam Altman]]
- **Damages sought:** $150B payable to the nonprofit arm
- **Relief sought:** removal of Altman and Greg Brockman
- Accused Altman of lying based on witness testimony; framed [[entities/Microsoft]] partnership as complicit
- OpenAI's counter: Musk knew about for-profit plans since 2018, wanted Tesla/xAI control, has "selective amnesia"
- Closing arguments wrapped 2026-05-15

### Outcome (2026-05-18) — Musk lost
A unanimous jury **dismissed all claims** after **<2 hours** of deliberation, finding the suit fell outside a **3-year statute of limitations** (brought too late) — the merits of the charitable-trust claim were never reached. Musk posted that the decision was a "calendar technicality" and said he will **appeal**. ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/5/18/elon-musk-loses-lawsuit-against-openai), [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/openai-elon-musk-case-verdict-rcna345655))

## Strategic Stakes

A Musk win *could* have forced OpenAI restructuring ahead of its rumored $1T IPO — instead the dismissal **removes that obstacle** (pending appeal). Because the verdict turned on timing rather than merits, it sets weak precedent on the underlying charitable-origins-to-for-profit question, which remains legally untested.
