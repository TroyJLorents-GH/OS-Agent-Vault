# Anthropic

AI safety company and developer of [[Claude]]. Founded with an explicit focus on [[alignment]] — building AI systems that are safe, beneficial, and understandable.

## 81K Study

In December 2025, Anthropic conducted what they believe is the **largest and most multilingual qualitative study ever conducted** — 80,508 Claude.ai users across 159 countries and 70 languages interviewed via [[Anthropic Interviewer]]. Key outputs:

- Identified the [[light and shade of AI]] framework — benefits and harms are entangled, not separate
- Mapped 9 clusters of what people want from AI and 13 categories of concerns
- Found that **economic displacement is the strongest predictor of AI sentiment** — more than existential risk or governance
- Found that [[cognitive atrophy]] is worst in institutional settings and minimal when learning is volitional
- Described this as "a new form of social science" — qualitative research at massive scale

### Research Initiatives Referenced
- **Anthropic Interviewer:** Claude-based conversational research tool
- **Beneficial Deployments program:** collaborations with AI for Science and nonprofit partners
- **Economic policy research:** updating their thinking on AI's economic impacts
- **Wellbeing follow-up study:** measuring Claude's effects on people's wellbeing over time

## Products & Tools

- **[[Claude]]** — conversational AI assistant (main product)
- **[[Claude.ai]]** — web interface for Claude
- **[[Anthropic Interviewer]]** — research tool: Claude prompted to conduct structured interviews with adaptive follow-ups
- **Claude-powered classifiers** — used internally for large-scale qualitative analysis

## Relevance

Anthropic's public research and transparency about user behavior and concerns is unusually detailed for an AI lab. The 81K study is a valuable primary source for understanding how real people experience AI — not just technologists' projections.
