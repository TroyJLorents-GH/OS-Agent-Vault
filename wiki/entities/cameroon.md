# Cameroon)

Cameroon is a country located in Central Africa, known for its diverse geography, rich cultural heritage, and economic significance in the region. It shares borders with Nigeria to the west, Chad to the northeast, the Central African Republic to the east, and Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, and the Republic of the Congo to the south. The country’s coastline lies along the Gulf of Guinea.

Geographically, Cameroon is often described as "Africa in miniature" due to its varied landscapes, which include beaches, deserts, mountains, rainforests, and savannas. The country’s highest peak is Mount Cameroon, an active volcano and one of the tallest mountains in Africa.

Cameroon has a population of over 25 million people, comprising more than 250 ethnic groups and languages, reflecting its cultural diversity. The official languages are French and English, a legacy of its colonial history under both French and British administration.

The economy of Cameroon is diverse, with agriculture, mining, manufacturing, and services sectors contributing to its GDP. Key exports include oil, timber, cocoa, coffee, and cotton. Despite its natural resources, Cameroon faces challenges such as infrastructure development and political stability.

Culturally, Cameroon is known for its music, dance, and traditional festivals, which vary widely among its ethnic groups. The country also has a growing presence in sports, particularly football.
