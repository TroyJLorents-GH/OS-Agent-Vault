# Google

Google LLC is an American multinational technology company specializing in Internet-related services and products, including online advertising technologies, search engine, cloud computing, software, and hardware. Founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin, Google has grown to become one of the world's most influential technology companies.

In the realm of AI-powered educational tools, Google has developed products such as [[NotebookLM]], an AI-driven note-taking assistant designed to help students organize and generate study materials from their documents. NotebookLM integrates AI features like chat assistance and content summarization, positioning itself as a competitor to similar offerings like Adobe's Acrobat Spaces.

Google's continuous innovation in AI and educational technology reflects its commitment to enhancing productivity and learning experiences through intelligent automation and user-centered design. Its tools often leverage AI grounding in source documents to ensure accuracy and relevance in generated content.

## AI Position (2026)

Google (via DeepMind) is one of three frontier labs alongside [[Anthropic]] and [[OpenAI]]. Its flagship is the **[[Gemini]]** model family — natively multimodal, tiered into Pro / Flash / Flash-Lite. Unique structural advantage: **distribution at planetary scale** — new models deploy across Search, Maps, YouTube, Docs, Gmail, and Chrome on day one, a surface no rival can match.

### Gemini cadence
Google treats the Flash tier as a continuous software product, shipping sub-quarterly: Gemini 3 Flash (Dec 2025) → 3.1 Pro (Feb 2026) → 3.1 Flash-Lite (Mar 2026) → **3.5 Flash** (I/O, May 19 2026). The pre-I/O leak called it "3.2 Flash" and claimed Flash-tier output matching 3.1 Pro at ~1/6 cost — a pure [[Inference Economics]] play. **The thesis held; the version number didn't.** See [[Gemini]] and [[S — Gemini 3.2 Flash Before Google IO 2026]].

### Google I/O 2026 (May 19) — what shipped
Confirmed ([Google](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/ai/google-io-2026-all-our-announcements/), [9to5Google](https://9to5google.com/2026/05/19/google-io-2026-news/)): **Gemini 3.5 Flash GA** (beats 3.1 Pro on coding/agentic/multimodal; ~4× faster output) via **Google Antigravity** + Gemini API in AI Studio & Android Studio · **Gemini 3.5 Pro** in testing (GA ~June) · **[[Gemma]] 4** open weights (Apache 2.0 — see [[Open-Source AI]]) · Android 17 AI Core · Project Astra · Android XR smart glasses.
