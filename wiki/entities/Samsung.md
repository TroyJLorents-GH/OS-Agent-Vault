# Samsung

South Korean conglomerate; Samsung Electronics is one of the world's top memory chip producers and a critical node in the AI hardware supply chain.

## 18-Day Strike (planned May 21, 2026) — AVERTED

- **45,000+ workers** prepared to strike
- **Trigger:** bonus disparity tied to AI-driven profitability
  - Memory chip division: up to **607% of annual salary** bonus (AI demand driving record profits)
  - Logic / foundry division: **50–100%** (losses)
- Union framing: stark internal inequity, talent flight to SK Hynix
- **Analyst estimate (if it had run):** $14–21B operating profit hit
- **Downstream risk:** memory chip supply for AI data centers, smartphones, laptops

### Resolution (2026-05-20) — last-minute deal
After **government-mediated** negotiation, the union approved a tentative wage deal with **~74% support**, calling off the 18-day strike and ending a **5-month labor dispute**. Terms tie bonuses to operating profit; some semiconductor employees could see bonuses **exceeding 500M won (~$340K)**. Strike risk vanishing helped Samsung Electronics shares jump ~8%. **Internal tensions persist** — the chip vs non-chip compensation gap that triggered the dispute is narrowed, not closed. ([Washington Times](https://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2026/may/20/samsungs-union-putting-strike-reaching-last-minute-wage-deal/), [The Week](https://www.theweek.in/news/biz-tech/2026/05/22/south-korea-samsung-labour-dispute-update.html))

## Strategic Significance

Samsung memory is central to AI inference economics (high-bandwidth memory is a bottleneck for serving large models). A protracted strike compounds the supply-side fragility the AI boom is already exposing. See [[Inference Economics]].
