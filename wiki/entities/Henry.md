# Henry (ai.work)

**Henry** is an "AI worker" for IT operations, built by **ai.work** (CEO/co-founder **Maor Ezer**). Positioned not as a chatbot or a feature, but as a **workforce-layer agent** that does real, repetitive IT work end-to-end — pitch: "5× faster teams in just weeks."

## What it claims to do

Behaves like an employee rather than software:
- Logs into apps, reads/writes data, handles exceptions
- Executes multi-step tasks across **5–15 systems per workflow**
- Adapts as tools and policies change; self-heals failing sequences
- Acts as a **system of action** on top of existing systems of record (ITSM, ERP, HRIS, CRM)
- Escalates to humans only when judgment is required (purposeful human-in-the-loop)

This is the product embodiment of the [[AI Workforce]] thesis laid out in [[S — Henry AI Worker Enterprise Predictions 2026]].

## Category

AI-native enterprise execution platform — the "buy" side of Maor Ezer's prediction that DIY internal AI is too brittle/expensive for most enterprises (build where you differentiate, buy where you accelerate). Competes with the agentic-automation wave: Microsoft Copilot Cowork, ServiceNow/ITSM-embedded agents, and horizontal RPA-successor startups. See [[Agentic AI]].
