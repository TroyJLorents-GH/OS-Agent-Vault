# OpenAI

AI research lab and product company. Originally a nonprofit; restructured into a capped-profit / for-profit hybrid. Central player in the 2026 LLM landscape alongside [[entities/anthropic]] and Google DeepMind.

## Products & Models

- **GPT-5.4** — scores 83% on GDPVal (matches human experts on economically valuable tasks). See [[AI Industry Landscape April 2026]].
- **ChatGPT** — consumer assistant; integrated into [[entities/Apple]] Siri in 2024
- **Codex** — coding agent. As of May 2026, available on ChatGPT mobile (iPhone, iPad, Android). Manages tasks across laptops, dev boxes, remote envs. See [[AI Coding Tools]].
- **Sora** — video generation, **shut down**; resources redirected to robotics ([[Physical AI]]). Compute economics didn't work at scale. (Source: [[sources/S — Best AI Tools 2026]])

## Legal / Governance Pressure

### Musk v. OpenAI (Oakland Federal) — RESOLVED, OpenAI cleared
- [[entities/Elon Musk]] sued OpenAI and [[entities/Sam Altman]] for allegedly abandoning nonprofit roots
- Damages sought: **$150B** payable to the nonprofit arm; also sought removal of Altman + Greg Brockman
- Musk's side: accused Altman of lying; called [[entities/Microsoft]] partnership complicit
- OpenAI's side: Musk knew about for-profit plans since 2018; wanted Tesla/xAI control; "selective amnesia"
- **Verdict (2026-05-18):** unanimous jury **dismissed all claims** after deliberating <2 hours. Decided on a **3-year statute of limitations** (filed too late) — did *not* reach the merits of the charitable-trust claim. Altman, Brockman, and OpenAI **not liable**. Musk called it a "calendar technicality" and vowed to appeal. ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/18/nx-s1-5822366/musk-altman-openai-jury-verdict-claims-dismissed), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/musk-altman-openai-trial-verdict.html))
- **Net effect:** legal cloud over the nonprofit→for-profit restructuring lifts (pending appeal) — clears a path toward the rumored $1T IPO rather than forcing restructuring

### Apple Friction
Reportedly exploring legal options against [[entities/Apple]] over how the Siri partnership has unfolded. Tension reflects who owns the user relationship — platform vs model provider.
