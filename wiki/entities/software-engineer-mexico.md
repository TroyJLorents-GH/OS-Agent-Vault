# Software engineer (Mexico)

Software engineers in Mexico are part of a growing and dynamic technology sector influenced by global trends in artificial intelligence (AI) and digital transformation. According to a large-scale qualitative study conducted by [[Anthropic]], which surveyed over 80,000 users across 159 countries and 70 languages, software engineers in Mexico share many of the global community’s hopes and concerns regarding AI.

The study identified nine primary themes reflecting users' desires for AI, including professional excellence, personal transformation, societal impact, and creative expression. For Mexican software engineers, AI represents both an opportunity to enhance productivity and innovation, and a challenge that raises questions about job security, ethical use, and societal implications.

Mexican software engineers often engage with AI technologies for tasks such as AI-assisted productivity, AI-enabled learning, and AI-driven entrepreneurship. Their experiences highlight a nuanced balance between optimism about AI’s potential to improve professional workflows and anxieties about its broader effects on the labor market and society.

This perspective aligns with broader qualitative research trends emphasizing real-world user experiences over abstract debates. Mexican professionals contribute to and benefit from conversational AI tools, open-ended interviews, and sentiment analysis techniques that inform AI development tailored to diverse cultural and linguistic contexts.

Overall, software engineers in Mexico are integral to the evolving AI landscape, navigating the interplay between technological advancement and societal impact while fostering innovation within the country’s expanding tech ecosystem.
