# Freelancer (USA)

Freelancer (USA) refers to independent professionals in the United States who engage with artificial intelligence (AI) technologies as part of their work and personal development. Insights into this group's interaction with AI were highlighted in a large-scale qualitative study conducted by [[Anthropic]], which surveyed over 80,000 users across 159 countries and 70 languages, including a significant representation from the USA.

The study explored freelancers’ hopes, fears, and experiences with AI, revealing a multifaceted relationship characterized by both optimism and concern. American freelancers, like their global counterparts, expressed desires for AI to enhance professional excellence, support creative expression, and enable personal transformation. They also voiced anxieties about AI’s societal impact and potential challenges in their industries.

Key themes identified among freelancers include AI-assisted productivity, AI-driven entrepreneurship, and AI-enabled learning, reflecting their reliance on AI tools to improve efficiency, innovate business models, and acquire new skills. The research underscores the importance of understanding real-world user perspectives, moving beyond theoretical debates to address practical implications of AI adoption in freelance work.

This nuanced understanding of Freelancer (USA) highlights the evolving role of AI in shaping independent work, emphasizing both the opportunities and challenges faced by freelancers navigating an AI-integrated professional landscape.
