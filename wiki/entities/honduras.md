# Honduras

Honduras is a country located in Central America, bordered by Guatemala, El Salvador, Nicaragua, the Pacific Ocean, and the Caribbean Sea. It is known for its rich natural resources, diverse ecosystems, and cultural heritage influenced by indigenous peoples, Spanish colonization, and Afro-Caribbean communities.

The capital city is Tegucigalpa, which serves as the political and economic center. Honduras has a population of approximately 10 million people, with Spanish as the official language. The country’s economy is primarily based on agriculture, manufacturing, and services, with key exports including coffee, bananas, and textiles.

Honduras has a presidential representative democratic republic system, with a president serving as both head of state and government. The nation faces challenges such as poverty, crime, and political instability, but it also has ongoing efforts toward economic development and social progress.

Culturally, Honduras is known for its traditional music, dance, and festivals, as well as archaeological sites like the ancient Mayan city of Copán. The country’s biodiversity includes tropical rainforests, mountains, and coastal reefs, making it a destination for ecotourism.
